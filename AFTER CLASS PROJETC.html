Facts About Christmas

1. Christmas is a major festival celebrated on 25th December every year to commemorate the birth of Jesus Christ. Christians believe Jesus Christ to be the Son of God who came to spread the message of love and peace. This festival holds great religious importance and is celebrated with devotion and happiness. 2. Christmas is celebrated in many countries across the world, not only by Christians but also by people of other religions. It brings people together and creates an atmosphere of joy, harmony, and goodwill. People greet one another with “Merry Christmas” and share happiness. 3. Decorating the Christmas tree is one of the most popular traditions of Christmas. The tree is decorated with lights, stars, bells, and colorful ornaments. It symbolizes hope, life, and celebration during the festive season. 4. Santa Claus is an important symbol of Christmas, especially for children. He is believed to bring gifts for children who behave well throughout the year. The character of Santa Claus is inspired by Saint Nicholas, who was famous for his kindness and generosity.

5. Exchanging gifts and greeting cards is a common practice during Christmas. Families and friends gather together to celebrate the festival. Homes are decorated, and people enjoy spending quality time with their loved ones. 6. Special dishes like cakes, cookies, sweets, and plum cake are prepared during Christmas. Many people attend church services, especially the midnight mass on Christmas Eve, to offer prayers and sing carols. 7. Christmas teaches important values such as love, forgiveness, kindness, and sharing with others. It reminds people to help the poor and spread joy and peace. The festival encourages everyone to be compassionate and caring.
